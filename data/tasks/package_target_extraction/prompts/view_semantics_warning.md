[CRITICAL VIEW-SEMANTICS WARNING]
This is an electronic component drawing. Do not determine a view’s type from its position on the page. A view’s page position is only a layout fact; it does not, by itself, define the physical perspective of the view or the physical meaning of that view’s dimensions.

Do not assume that once a view is treated as a top, side, end, or principal view, its sheet X-axis and sheet Y-axis automatically have fixed package-dimension semantics. In electronic component drawings, view orientation may be rotated under glass-box projection conventions, so the physical meanings carried by the on-sheet horizontal and vertical directions may be exchanged.

When projection relationships are present, first-angle and third-angle conventions may provide evidence for the horizontal/vertical semantic correspondence between views. However, page placement alone is never sufficient evidence for view identity or axis meaning.
